PIVOT

Policy Improvement via Trust-Region Optimization from Deployment Experience

Anonymous Author(s)

Under double-blind review

01Approach

“For the things we have to learn before we can do them, we learn by doing them.”

— Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics

“Vigorous action deepens knowledge; deep knowledge perfects action.”

— Zhang Shi, Commentary on the Analects

We bring this idea to embodied foundation models: a robot that learns by doing. Deployed in the real world, our policy improves itself round after round from its own deployment experience — demonstrations, human corrections, and autonomous rollouts alike — turning each into steady, monotonic gains. We call this system PIVOT.

02Tasks

Task Demonstrations

After post-training, a single policy runs unattended over long horizons across contact-rich bimanual tasks, each requiring fine-grained contact control and recovery from small execution errors.

03Generalization

Generalization

We probe visual generalization on objects with appearances never seen during training: the Laundry policy folds unseen T-shirts, and the Zip-Tie policy threads colored cable ties. Both indicate that the learned contact-rich skills transfer beyond the training distribution.

04Robustness

Robustness

We stress-test our post-trained policies against unexpected mishaps. The Paper Box policy makes robust corrections to small accidents mid-assembly, while the Laundry policy resists physical disturbances from a human, recognizes and re-does a poor fold, and recovers from small execution slips mid-fold.
